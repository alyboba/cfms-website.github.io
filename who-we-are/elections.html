DIRECTOR OF EDUCATION

  • Maylynn Ding

    I’m Maylynn, a final year student at McMaster, and I’m asking for your support to serve as the next VP Education. My involvement with the CFMS began in first-year as the Education Committee Vice-Chair and this past year, I had the privilege of serving as an Ontario representive and attaché to the Education portfolio. In this role, I had the incredible opportunity to represent medical students on AFMC and CaRMS committees, and provide feedback on key reports. Anyone who knows me knows I love the CFMS, and the Education portfolio is especially close to my heart. 250 words cannot do it justice—so please find me and ask me questions. As VP Education, one of my priorities will be to further our work in supporting unmatched Canadian medical students. This is the first year the unmatched student network was run and we will look into methods of expanding it sustainably while respecting privacy. Competency-based medical education (CBME) is another big topic. As more residency programs adopt it, there is much to be done in understanding how best to prepare students in transitioning into residency and the role of CBME at the undergraduate level. Lastly, I hope to continue the CFMS’ ongoing advocacy efforts to improve the AFMC electives portal, CaRMS Online, and learner privacy. The medical education landscape is constantly evolving and medical students must be included in the important conversations taking place. I hope you will trust me to be the strong, reliable voice we need at the table.

    I have greatly appreciated the opportunity to contribute to the CFMS and hope that you will give me the honour of serving as your Vice-President Finance for the 2018-2019 year.

    Regards,

    Henry

DIRECTOR OF STUDENT AFFAIRS

  • Victor Do

    While being a medical student comes with much excitement and privilege, coping with long hours, stressful working and learning environments (which often are not fully supportive of learners), challenges of patient care and balancing family and personal commitments are a daunting task. That upwards of one-third of pre-clerkship students and half of clerks report significant burnout is only one measure that shows we must continue to improve student supports. Our members should not graduate with mountains of debt, feeling burnt out and having experienced mistreatment from other medical colleagues.

    I want to be your Director of Student Affairs for the 2018-2019 year so that we can continue advocating for a health-promoting working and learning environment for all medical trainees. We must continue to raise awareness and de-stigmatize the reality that we all face mental, physical and other health challenges during medical school. Our programming must be more comprehensive (including our responsibility to continue pursuing and improving existing student discounts/partnerships that support our education, travel and more) and we must continue to build resilience through unique personal development opportunities. I have had the pleasure of leading the development and implementation of the National Wellness Program and I yearn for the opportunity to further develop this project with other national stakeholders, all medical faculties and most importantly you, the students, who deserve a CFMS student affairs teams that advocates relentlessly for you. It would be an honour to support, connect and represent you this year.

  • Emily Macphail

    Dear CFMS members,

    My name is Emily, and I am the current National Officer of Wellness for CFMS, as well as a University of Calgary MD/MSc. student (currently final year MSc.; clerkship begun). I am requesting your support in the Director of Student Affairs election.

    If selected for the honour of representing you as Director SA in the coming year, my vision is for CFMS to focus on medical trainee support and wellness at a systemic level, targeting the following primary areas:

    2) Improved resources for clerks.

    -Advocacy for away-elective wellness supports (from both elective schools and home schools via distance).

    -Support for unmatched CMGS (e.g. network of mentors, school-provided options).

    -Increased CaRMS preparation resources.

    3) Optimization/sustainability of the CFMS Student Affairs portfolio expansion.

    -Continue to grow the Longitudinal Wellness Initiative, the mistreatment and students with disabilities files, Safe Spaces, etc.

    -Support positions through creation/transition: National Officer of Services/Services Committee, and National Officer of Wellness (as role changes to reflect governance).
